Common Dental Emergencies

Learn what to do in case of a dental emergency before you arrive at the dental lab.

Severe Toothache

Rinse your mouth with warm salt water and use dental floss to remove any lodged food. Apply a cold compress to the outside of your cheek if there is swelling. Never apply aspirin directly to the gum.

Knocked-Out Tooth

Retrieve the tooth, holding it by the crown (top part). Gently rinse the root if dirty, but do not scrub or remove any tissue fragments. If possible, gently insert the tooth back into the socket, or keep it in milk.

Broken Crown or Veneer

Locate the broken fragment or loose crown. Keep it safe and call us immediately. Avoid chewing on that side of your mouth to prevent further damage to the exposed tooth structure.
